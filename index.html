About the Workshop

Why Agentic AI, Why Now?

Agentic AI systems can independently perform complex tasks, make decisions, and adapt to changing contexts. You may already be using them — in research assistants like Elicit and Consensus, or in tools like ChatGPT and Claude for deep research and data analysis. This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners from across disciplines to collectively explore what this means for science and society.

01

Establish a shared understanding of agentic AI concepts, architectures, and current capabilities across life sciences domains.

02

Identify concrete opportunities and barriers to integrating agentic AI into research workflows, from data analysis to academic writing.

03

Explore domain-specific use cases — greenhouse horticulture, bioinformatics, digital health, agri-food — and what responsible deployment looks like.

04

Co-create a shared research agenda that bridges academia and industry, informing a second in-person workshop in August 2026.

Programme

Workshop Agenda

A carefully balanced mix of expert talks and hands-on interactive sessions, designed so that the majority of time is spent doing, not just listening.

13:00
Welcome & Live Pulse Poll Interactive
Organisers open the session. A live Mentimeter/Slido poll gives an immediate snapshot of the room's AI experience — results shared instantly to set the stage.
13:15
Talk 1 — Systems Thinking & Agentic AI Architecture Talk
Bedir Tekinerdogan (WUR) · How systems engineering principles apply to designing reliable agentic pipelines.
13:30
Talk 2 — Runtime Governance of AI Agents Talk
Javed Vassilis Khan (Sappi / Wiser-Mark) · Compliance, guardrails, and audit trails for agents in production.
13:45
Talk 3 — Agentic AI at Scale: Spotify & Shell Talk
Mohamed Elkady (Spotify) & Osama Mazhar (Shell) · Back-to-back lightning format. GenAI in cloud-scale production and industrial workflows.
14:00
Talk 4 — Agentic AI for Agri-Food & Digital Health Talk
Anand Gavai (EIC Accelerator Evaluator) · Real use cases across digital health and agri-food; bridging research and industry.
14:10
Talk 5 — AI for Greenhouse Horticulture Horticulture
Pinglin Zhang (Wageningen Research) · Machine learning and sensor integration for greenhouse environments.
14:20
Break + Breakout Briefing Break
10-minute break. Facilitator explains the Lightning Decision Jam format and participants move to their pre-assigned Zoom breakout rooms.
14:30
Breakout Sessions — Lightning Decision Jam Interactive
4–5 parallel groups work on structured challenge canvases in Miro. Each group applies the same LDJ method to a different theme. AI tools (Claude, Elicit, Miro AI) available throughout.
15:15
Group Report-Back & Synthesis Interactive
Each group presents a 3-minute summary. Key insights are posted to a shared Miro board and the full group votes using Dotmocracy to surface the top priorities.
15:45
Closing: Outcomes & Next Steps Closing
Organiser synthesises live results. Announcement of in-person follow-up workshop (August 2026). QR code for post-workshop survey. Commitment cards.
