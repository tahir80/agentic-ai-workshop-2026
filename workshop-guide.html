Phase 1 · Affinity Map

Affinity Map

⏱ 10 minutes
Objective

Surface the problems participants care about and cluster them to reveal shared themes.

🖥 Open Miro Board ↗
1
Pose the question

Ask participants: "What is one problem related to Agentic AI you'd like to work on today?"

2
Silent writing — 5 min

Everyone writes one problem on a sticky note. Silent, no discussion. One problem only — not a topic or solution.

3
Post all at once

Everyone places their sticky note on the Miro board simultaneously.

4
Cluster together — 3 min

As a group, drag similar problems together into clusters. Duplicates stay — they show shared concerns.

5
Name the clusters — 2 min

Give each cluster a short label. Don't debate names — good enough is fine.

📌 Note

Point out patterns to the group: "Notice that X and Y came up multiple times — these are shared concerns."

Start on a Positive Note

⏱ 10 minutes
Objective

Open with what's working well before moving into problems.

🖥 Open Miro Board ↗
1
Silent writing — 4 min

Participants write what is working well, one item per sticky note.

2
Share & post — 6 min

Each person reads their notes aloud and places them on the top half of the sailboat canvas. No interruptions.

📌 Note

These notes are not carried forward. Their purpose is to set a constructive tone.

Capture Problems

⏱ 5 minutes
Objective

Get all problems, frustrations, and concerns on the board — silently and without judgement.

1
Silent writing — 4 min

Participants write problems, frustrations, or concerns. One per sticky note. As many as possible.

2
Post all at once — 1 min

Everyone places notes on the bottom half of the sailboat simultaneously. No reading aloud.

Phase 2 · Lightning Decision Jam
LDJ Step 3 of 8

Prioritize Problems

⏱ 3 minutes
Objective

Surface the most important problems through silent dot voting — no debate.

1
Each participant gets 3 votes

Use Miro's voting feature or dot stickers. Vote silently and individually.

2
Arrange by votes

After voting, order problems from most to least votes. The top problem moves forward.

📌 Note

Participants can vote on their own notes and stack multiple votes on one item.

Phase 2 · Lightning Decision Jam
LDJ Step 4 of 8

Reframe as "How Might We"

⏱ 3 minutes
Objective

Turn the top-voted problem into an open, actionable challenge question.

1
Take the top-voted problem

Rewrite it as: "How Might We [solve this]?"

2
Display it prominently

Post the HMW question on the Miro board where everyone can see it for the rest of the session.

📌 Why HMW?

How = action is possible · Might = many solutions exist · We = collaborative effort.

Ideate Solutions

⏱ 6 minutes
Objective

Generate as many solutions to the HMW question as possible — quantity over quality.

1
Silent writing — 5 min

One idea per sticky note. Aim for breadth — no filtering, no discussion.

2
Post all at once — 1 min

Place notes on the board simultaneously. Each note must be understandable without explanation.

Prioritize Solutions

⏱ 5 minutes
Objective

Vote to identify the most promising solutions.

1
Each participant gets 6 votes — 4 min

Vote silently on the solutions that best address the HMW. Distribute votes however you like.

2
Reorder by vote count

Arrange solutions from most to fewest votes. Solutions with only 1 vote are set aside.

Effort / Impact Scale

⏱ 10 minutes
Objective

Plot solutions on a 2×2 grid to find the sweet spot: high impact, low effort.

1
Place each top-voted solution on the grid

For each one, ask: "Higher or lower impact? More or less effort?" — keep each discussion under 20 seconds.

2
Mark the sweet spot

Solutions in the top-left are your priority. Mark them with a star or coloured dot.

Phase 2 · Lightning Decision Jam
LDJ Step 8 of 8

Make It Actionable

⏱ 5 minutes
Objective

Turn sweet-spot solutions into assigned, concrete actions that can start immediately.

1
Define 3 action steps per solution

The first step must be easy and doable right away. All steps completable within 1–2 weeks.

2
Assign one owner per solution

One name, not a group. Place action steps next to the solution on the board.

3
Export & share

Screenshot or export the Miro board and share with all participants.
