Breakout Sessions

45 minutes of focused group work, followed by a collective synthesis. All groups use the same Lightning Decision Jam method — only the challenge question changes.

The Method

Lightning Decision Jam

All breakout groups follow the same structured format: the Lightning Decision Jam (LDJ). Developed by AJ&Smart and used by teams at Google, Airbnb, and the UN, LDJ is a rapid, democratic method that takes a group from open-ended problems to a prioritised action list in under an hour — no endless debate, no dominant voices. 1
Individual Problem Writing 5 min
Everyone writes problems or challenges on sticky notes — one idea per note, silently and independently. This prevents anchoring bias and ensures every voice contributes before discussion begins.
2
Share & Present 5 min
Each person briefly presents their notes (30 sec each). No discussion or rebuttals yet — just sharing. Facilitator groups similar ideas together on the Miro board.
3
Dot Vote on Problems 5 min
Each participant gets 5 dots to distribute across the problems they find most important. Dots can be stacked on one note or spread out. The top-voted problems move forward.
4
Reframe as "How Might We…?" 5 min
The facilitator reframes the top problem as a "How Might We" question. This simple reframe turns a complaint into an opportunity and opens up the solution space without prescribing an answer. Example: "We can't trust LLM outputs" → "How might we verify and audit LLM decisions in a research workflow?"
5
Generate Solutions — Silently 10 min
Participants write solutions to the HMW question, again independently and silently. You can use Claude or ChatGPT here to brainstorm — paste the HMW question into the AI and add its suggestions to your own notes.
6
Vote on Solutions 10 min
Solutions are presented and dot-voted again. The top-voted solutions surface the group's collective priorities without lengthy debate.
7
Define Priority Actions 5 min
The group turns the winning solution(s) into 1–2 concrete, actionable next steps. These become the group's contribution to the plenary synthesis and to the research agenda for the August 2026 workshop.

Tools & Platforms

What You'll Use

Main platform for the full workshop and for splitting into parallel breakout rooms. The facilitator can broadcast to all rooms simultaneously.

Collaboration
✨

Miro's built-in AI features can cluster sticky notes, generate summaries of the board, and suggest related ideas — useful for the synthesis step at the end of LDJ.
AI · Built-in
🤖
Claude / ChatGPT
Use during the silent solution-generation step (Step 5) to brainstorm additional ideas. Paste your "How Might We" question in and add the AI's suggestions to the board alongside your own.
AI · Brainstorming
Using AI tools during the session

You are encouraged to use Claude, ChatGPT, or Elicit during the breakout — especially during Step 5 (silent solution generation). The goal is not to outsource thinking to the AI, but to use it as a sparring partner: give it your "How Might We" question, see what it suggests, critically evaluate those suggestions, and add the ones you find genuinely useful to the board. This mirrors how agentic AI works in practice — the human stays in the loop.
